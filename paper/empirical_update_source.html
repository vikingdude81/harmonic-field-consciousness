Life Before Language — Empirical Update to the Reference

A companion to the level-by-level reference. Where the reference posed open questions and testable predictions, this update reports what a season of measurement found. Every number traces to the project's PROBE_MATRIX. Read each level's update beside the reference's own treatment of that level.

Alex Bone · Independent Researcher · July 2026

How to read this update

The reference was written before the experiments. It named the sharpest testable questions the framework generates and, for each level, the precise way its probes fall short. This companion adds, per level, three things: the claim as the reference stated it, what was measured, and the refined claim. Two of the updates do more than confirm the framework — they change it, and those are marked REFINEMENT.

One discipline carries throughout, unchanged from the reference: every result is small-scale, the probes are design-diverse but built within one research lineage (not independent samples), and nothing measured crosses the wall. The experiments locate the wall; they do not dissolve it.

The probe roster, updated

The reference describes three probes. There are now four measured ones plus a flagship experiment:

  • Gabriel's Horn (Level 7) — entropy-decay decoding. Measured: pure modulation.
  • LGI / spectral geometry (Level 4) — forbidden-subspace regulation. The substrate for the flagship.
  • Holographic RAG / FTTF (Level 5) — superposed memory. Measured: presence-tax, content-indifference. (New probe; the reference had no Level-5 system.)
  • Continuant Probe (Level 3) — formerly "Oracle Prime"; the evolutionary runtime plus a new within-life complement. Measured: bearer-indexing dose curve. ("LayeredFitness" is dropped — it named no actual implementation.)

The flagship, cutting across Levels 4 and 6, is the construction-order experiment: the same regulatory geometry introduced at three different points in a model's formation.

Level 7 — Reason and language

Reference claim. AI imagination is disciplined by settings; Gabriel's Horn moves regulation from a static knob to a structured schedule, an implementable rung of the temperature ladder.

Measured. Applying the schedule to a small instruction model and testing competence on unrelated tasks, the schedule shaped output with no measurable effect on competence (ablation effect within confidence of zero). Because an inference-time decoder never touches the weights, removing it restores the bit-identical model.

Refined claim. For regulation applied at decoding, the "settings, not maturity" verdict is not merely observed but architectural: such a constraint shapes what the system does and cannot in principle change what it is. The temperature ladder's rung (ii) is confirmed as pure modulation.

Level 5 — Memory and development

Reference claim. Persistent state is real but "the store is available to the system, not constitutive of it"; genuine memory would require accumulation that modifies the accumulating system, and retrieval not separable from the retriever — "closer to engineering than it first appears."

Measured. A holographic memory (superposition — the closest formal analogue to sedimentation available) serving a small model gives a sharp dissociation on unrelated tasks. The memory layer's presence significantly degrades competence (retrieved context taxes attention). Corrupting the memory's content — wrong retrievals, no notification — has no measurable effect. The system is sensitive to having the service and indifferent to whether it is right.

Refined claim. "Available, not constitutive" now has a signature: presence-tax with content-indifference — the exact inverse of constitutive memory, where presence is transparent and corruption is devastating. This is what "connected to memory" rather than "constituted through memory" looks like when made measurable. (Qualification the reference would want: the sedimentation is real only for the compressed routing index, which has a measured capacity limit biological memory need not share.)

Level 4 — Sensory interface, and the sharpest question REFINEMENT

Reference claim. Reason is answerable to sensation in the human case; LGI added to a model yields a signal that is "input-to-consider, not condition-of-operation." Whether depth of integration ever converts modulation into answerability is "arguably the sharpest experimental question the framework generates," and LGI is "the closest available substrate for asking it."

Measured. The substrate was built (a spectral regularizer that projects a forbidden subspace out of a model's hidden states) and the question asked directly. The same geometry was introduced at three points in a model's history — attached at inference only, fine-tuned into a finished model, or present from the first training step — holding architecture, geometry, and data fixed. Three stable regimes resulted: the inference-only overlay is a tax (removing it improves competence — pure overhead); the fine-tuned retrofit is a crutch (the model leans on it; removal costs competence); the grown-in model is internalized (removal costs almost nothing, because the model formed within the allowed geometry and no longer needs the external projection). With the regulator entirely removed, the grown-in model's states occupy the forbidden subspace at 0.41–0.45 of the chance rate — the same value across two model sizes, two constraint fractions, two tokenizations, and seed replicates — while an unregulated model sits at chance.

Refined claim. The reference's binary — modulation or answerability — is too coarse. Deep formation under a constraint does not make the external constraint more necessary (the naive answerability reading); it makes it nearly removable while its discipline persists in the weights. That is a third thing, internalization, sitting between modulation and consequence. The sharpest question has an answer, and the answer adds a rung to the framework rather than settling a yes/no.

The retrofit asymmetry — permanence vs convergence REFINEMENT

Reference claim. Two respectable views — the asymmetry is permanent (construction order is load-bearing forever) or convergent (deep-enough retrofit collapses the distinction). "The honest answer is that we do not know"; the framework's job is to "make the question askable."

Measured. Fine-tuning the retrofit for increasing durations: at small scale, dependence on the layer decays toward a floor; at word level with adequate data, it does not decay at all. But the gap is constant where the dynamics are not — in every regime tested the adapted retrofit keeps roughly threefold the grown-in model's scaffold dependence, and no adaptation within range closes it.

Refined claim. Neither pole — a third option: path dependence with asymptotic non-convergence. Within the tested regimes, increased retrofit adaptation approached but did not reach the grown-in signature, and the residual gap is consistent with a construction-order dependence that adaptation does not close. The question is now not only askable but partially answered — at toy scale, honestly bounded. This is stronger for the asymmetry thesis than either original pole, because it is what the thesis predicts and the deflationary reading does not. (A free observation, on-message: the same pipeline's attempt to remove a trait from a finished model by the same geometric means succeeds at small scale and fails at larger scale — post-hoc construction scales worse exactly where grown-in construction scales better.)

Level 3 — Innate orientation, and the continuant

Reference claim. The evolutionary probe (called "Oracle Prime" in the reference) is real consequence — individuals that fail don't propagate — but the discipline operates on the lineage, not within a life. No individual has hunger, fear, or attachment as its own present condition.

Measured. The evolutionary machinery is the control case. Its complement was built: a single agent in a drifting world whose own outcomes update the parameters it acts with, against a yoked control altered by a different agent's outcomes — same rule, same information, wrong bearer. When outcomes are facts about the world, self-driven and other-driven alteration are indistinguishable: bearer-identity is idle. As outcomes are made to depend on the individual (a fixed private "taste" filtering what each agent experiences), the self-versus-yoked advantage grows monotonically and becomes universal across seeds.

Refined claim. The reference's "no individual has its own present condition" is now operational: bearer-identity becomes load-bearing exactly when outcomes stop being facts about the world and become facts of the bearer. This is the Level-3 claim — appetite is the organism's own condition, not a signal about a state — in measurable dress. It relocates the next question precisely: not "can outcomes alter the actor?" (yes, measured) but "can outcomes be constituted by the actor rather than assigned to it?" — which is the wall's Level-3 face. The reference's proposed rung-(iv) experiment (a persistent identity that bears its own outcomes) now exists as this probe's first result.

The wall, at higher resolution REFINEMENT

Reference claim. A constraint shapes what a system does; a consequence changes what a system is, and only a continuing something can have its being changed. The wall divides them.

Measured & refined. The grown-in model shows that a constraint present during formation genuinely changes what a system is — its competence and its state-space geometry come to embody a discipline it was formed under. By the reference's own criterion this is more than modulation. And yet nothing brought the system one step closer to stakes: it was changed; nothing mattered to it. So the near side of the wall has structure the original binary compressed:

modulation → internalization → ⟨ THE WALL ⟩ → consequence → subjectivity

The load-bearing addition: constitution and consequence-bearing are separable, and the grown-in model is the first concrete case of the former without the latter. The wall did not move when internalization was found above it; the frontier of what engineering reaches moved, and the wall was still there. That is the most the framework asks its probes to show — and, at small scale and with the appropriate caution, they now show it.

What is unchanged

Levels 1 and 2 remain framework posits; no experiment makes or needs a claim about them. The category error, the two gaps, the hallucination diagnostic, the bottom-up trajectory, and the retrofit-arrow reading of the whole map all stand exactly as the reference gives them. The measurements live entirely on the near side of the wall — Levels 4 through 7 and the operational approach to Level 3 — which is precisely where the framework said engineering could reach and characterization could work. The experiments are the framework doing what it said it would do: approach the wall from above, carefully, and report the shape of the asymptote.
