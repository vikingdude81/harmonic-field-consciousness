## 0. The thesis in one paragraph (updated)

The paper does NOT argue AI can't be conscious. It argues the debate happens at the wrong layer, separates the **engineering gap** (bridgeable) from the **metaphysical gap** (maybe not), and turns the wall into a boundary condition for research. NEW: the program has now been partially executed. Four probes + a flagship construction-order experiment measured the near side of the wall and found it has structure: **modulation → internalization → WALL → consequence → subjectivity**. Constitution and consequence-bearing are separable — formation can change what a system *is* without anything mattering *to* it. The wall did not move; the frontier did.

## 6. Overclaim watchlist (updated for the new results)

The internalization finding is the most overclaim-prone result the project has produced. Rehearsed discipline:
- Internalization is NOT answerability. The naive reading ("the model made the constraint its own, therefore the constraint matters to it") conflates constitution with consequence-bearing — the exact separation the result demonstrates.
- The 0.41–0.45 invariance is striking but is invariance *within one lineage at toy scale*. Say "stable across tested variations," never "universal."
- "Path dependence with asymptotic non-convergence" holds *within tested regimes*. The permanence question is narrowed, not closed.
- The presence-tax result depends on the specific holographic implementation; alternative memory architectures could in principle show different signatures. One probe ≠ the class.
- General rule carried over from v1: the local models flatter; the paper must not. (Hermes praise still excluded from all framing.)
